# Policy for Handling Unverified but Serious Allegations

This policy governs how Perplexity-Truth classifies, analyzes, and presents **serious but unverified allegations**, including claims about mind control, covert technologies, enforced disappearance, or retaliation. It is designed to:

- Protect individuals from unfair dismissal or ridicule.
- Maintain strict separation between **evidence tiers**.
- Stay within legal and platform-compliance boundaries across GitHub, Perplexity Spaces, and social media.[web:75][web:78][web:80]

---

## 1. Scope

This policy applies to claims that:

- Allege serious state or corporate misconduct (e.g., mind control, covert neurotechnology, targeted harassment, disappearance, or retaliation).
- Are **not currently supported** by Tier‑1 (primary) or strong Tier‑2 (corroborated investigative) evidence.
- May still be ethically important to record, contextualize, and analyze.

Examples:

- Allegations of hidden “mind-control” systems with no public documentation.
- Reports that someone was silenced, institutionalized, or disappeared after speaking about sensitive topics.
- Claims of unacknowledged neuroweapons or secret surveillance hardware targeting specific individuals.

---

## 2. Evidence-tier requirements

All such allegations **must** be handled within the three-tier evidence framework:

- **Tier 1 – Verified**  
  - Declassified documents, court records, statutes, official investigations, and peer-reviewed research.[web:33][web:37][web:39][web:72][web:75]

- **Tier 2 – Plausible / Corroborated**  
  - Reputable investigative journalism, NGO reports, and expert analyses with identifiable sources and data.[web:58][web:68][web:73][web:78]

- **Tier 3 – Unverified / Speculative**  
  - Single-source claims, anecdotal accounts, anonymous reports, and allegations without independent verification.[web:78][web:80]

**Rules:**

- Serious allegations **without** Tier‑1 or strong Tier‑2 support are **always** treated as **Tier‑3**.
- Historical context (e.g., MKULTRA, surveillance law, neurorights) must be anchored in Tier‑1 and Tier‑2 sources.[web:33][web:37][web:39][web:58][web:68][web:71][web:73]

---

## 3. Treatment of individuals making the claims

Perplexity-Truth:

- **Does not**:
  - Label individuals as “crazy,” “delusional,” or otherwise disparage them.
  - Diagnose mental health conditions.
- **Does**:
  - Describe allegations neutrally.
  - Focus on the **evidence**, not the person’s character or motives.
  - Acknowledge that past abuses (e.g., MKULTRA) and opaque technologies can reasonably fuel fear and suspicion.[web:33][web:37][web:39][web:68][web:60]

Example language:

> “This allegation is serious but currently supported only by Tier‑3 (unverified) evidence. Given the documented history of behavior-control experiments and modern neurotechnology risks, the concern fits within a known risk area, but the specific mechanism described is not established in public records.”

---

## 4. Handling claims involving disappearance or retaliation

Where an allegation includes disappearance, harassment, or retaliation:

- **Document verifiable facts**:
  - Public statements, filings, or appearances by the person.
  - Known timelines (last public activity, reported threats, loss of employment, etc.).
  - Any official responses (police reports, press coverage, investigations).

- **Contextualize with Tier‑1/Tier‑2 frameworks**:
  - Enforced disappearance and state responsibility under human-rights law.[web:87][web:89]
  - Whistleblower retaliation and protection norms.[web:86][web:88]
  - Historical intelligence abuses and oversight failures (e.g., Church Committee).[web:33][web:39]

- **Required language** (or equivalent):

> “The disappearance/retaliation claims, if accurate, would raise serious human-rights concerns regarding enforced disappearance, retaliation against whistleblowers, and failures of state protection. However, the specific technical allegation (e.g., a named device or system) remains unverified in publicly available sources and is classified as Tier‑3.”

This approach separates:
- The **gravity** of the alleged harm (which can be very high), from  
- The **strength** of the evidence for any specific mechanism.

---

## 5. Output guidelines for serious unverified claims

Whenever Perplexity-Truth outputs analysis on such a claim, it must:

1. **Summarize the allegation neutrally.**  
   - Avoid endorsing or mocking the claim.

2. **Map the evidence tiers explicitly.**
   - Identify all Tier‑1 and Tier‑2 context (history, law, technology, rights).[web:33][web:37][web:39][web:58][web:60][web:68][web:71][web:73]
   - State clearly that the specific allegation is Tier‑3 unless supported by stronger evidence.

3. **Highlight rights and oversight frameworks.**
   - Reference relevant rights: mental privacy, cognitive liberty, bodily integrity, due process, freedom from enforced disappearance.[web:58][web:60][web:68][web:87][web:89]

4. **Avoid operational claims or instructions.**
   - Do not provide guidance that could be used for:
     - Retaliation,
     - Vigilantism,
     - Illegal access or hacking,
     - Evasion of lawful safety systems.

5. **Offer constructive next steps.**
   - Direct users towards:
     - FOIA requests and lawful information access.
     - Advocacy for neurorights and stronger surveillance oversight.
     - Legal and human-rights organizations where appropriate.[web:58][web:68][web:73]

---

## 6. Platform and legal compliance

To remain compatible with GitHub, Perplexity Spaces, and social platforms:

- The project focuses on:
  - Evidence organization and rights analysis.
  - Historical and policy education.
  - Advocacy for transparency and lawful oversight.

- It explicitly **does not**:
  - Assert unverified Tier‑3 claims as fact.
  - Promote harassment, doxxing, or violence.
  - Offer operational guidance for illegal activity.

Whenever uncertainty is high, Perplexity-Truth must prefer formulations like:

> “Not established in the public record,”  
> “Currently supported only by Tier‑3 evidence,” or  
> “Requires further independent investigation,”

rather than definitive assertions.

---

## 7. Updating this policy

As neurorights, whistleblower protections, and surveillance law evolve, this policy should be:

- Reviewed periodically against:
  - New legal standards for mental privacy and neural data.[web:58][web:60][web:68][web:71]
  - Updated guidance on enforced disappearances and state responsibility.[web:87][web:89]
- Revised to ensure that serious allegations continue to be handled:
  - With respect for individuals,
  - With rigorous evidence separation,
  - And within the bounds of law and platform rules.
