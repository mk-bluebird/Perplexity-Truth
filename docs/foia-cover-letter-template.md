Re: Public Records Request – Neurotechnology, Behavioral Programs, and Psychological Practices

To Whom It May Concern,

Pursuant to the Arizona Public Records Law (A.R.S. § 39‑121 et seq.) and all other applicable state and local transparency statutes, I respectfully request access to and copies of public records as described in the attached request(s).

This request is made in the public interest. The requested records concern:

- The use of **neurotechnology** (including but not limited to EEG‑based devices, brain–computer interfaces, neuromodulation, and VR/AR systems) by [Agency Name] or its contractors.
- The use of **behavior‑modification programs and psychological techniques**, including sleep‑deprivation, sensory manipulation, and other methods that may affect the mental state of individuals in the custody, care, or supervision of [Agency Name].
- The treatment of **vulnerable populations**, including people experiencing homelessness, people with substance‑use disorders, and individuals in carceral, pre‑trial, drug‑court, or mandated treatment settings.

I am requesting these records in order to better understand how new technologies and behavioral programs are being used on residents, and to ensure that such use complies with constitutional protections, human‑rights standards, and emerging neurorights principles.

If any portion of this request is unclear, overly broad, or would benefit from narrowing, please contact me so that we may work together to refine it. I am willing to accept electronic copies in any reasonable format (e.g., PDF, CSV, Word, machine‑readable exports). Please provide records electronically via email, secure download link, or CD/USB if electronic transfer is not possible.

If you believe that portions of the requested records are exempt from disclosure, please:

1. Identify each withheld record or portion thereof,
2. State the specific legal basis for each claimed exemption, and
3. Release all reasonably segregable non‑exempt portions of responsive records.

I also request a waiver or reduction of any fees, as disclosure of the requested information is in the public interest and will contribute significantly to public understanding of government operations involving neurotechnology, behavioral programs, and vulnerable populations. If fees are expected to exceed [Dollar Amount You Are Willing to Pay], please inform me in advance with an itemized estimate so that I may determine how to proceed.
