### Section 1. Right to Mental Privacy, Cognitive Liberty, and Mental Integrity

(a) Every person has the right to **mental privacy**, including the right to be free from unauthorized access to, collection of, or interference with their brain activity, mental states, or thought processes by any public entity or any private entity acting on behalf of a public entity.

(b) Every person has the right to **cognitive liberty**, including the right to refuse any technological, pharmacological, or behavioral intervention designed to alter, manipulate, or condition their thoughts, emotions, perceptions, or decision‑making, except where a court of competent jurisdiction has authorized a specific intervention pursuant to strict scrutiny and with robust procedural safeguards.

(c) Every person has the right to **mental integrity and psychological continuity**, including the right not to be subjected, without their prior, informed, and voluntary consent, to any intervention that is reasonably likely to:

  (1) Induce confusion, paranoia, or a breakdown of personality;  
  (2) Disrupt sleep or circadian rhythms through intentional noise, light, or other environmental manipulation used as a means of coercion or control;  
  (3) Fragment, overwrite, or otherwise materially interfere with a person’s sense of personal identity or continuity of self.

### Section 2. Prohibition on Coercive Neurotechnology and Hypnotic Loop Practices

(a) No public entity, and no private entity acting on behalf of a public entity, shall deploy or use any device, system, or method that is designed or reasonably likely to:

  (1) Deliver persistent, repetitive, or subliminal audio, visual, electromagnetic, or other stimuli targeted at a specific individual or small group with the intent to destabilize, humiliate, or coerce them;  
  (2) Create or reinforce false beliefs about threats, loyalties, or relationships through hidden or undisclosed technological means;  
  (3) Induce or prolong sleep deprivation, sensory overload, or sensory deprivation as a means of punishment, interrogation, behavior control, or compliance.

(b) For purposes of this section, “hypnotic loop practice” means any covert, repetitive, or automated stimulus or sequence of stimuli, delivered through physical, digital, or virtual systems, that is intended to modify an individual’s mental state or behavior without their informed and contemporaneous awareness of the method being used.

(c) Any such practice is presumptively deemed a violation of this State’s prohibition on cruel, inhuman, or degrading treatment, and any evidence obtained thereby shall be inadmissible in any judicial or administrative proceeding.

### Section 3. Safeguards for Research and Vulnerable Populations

(a) No research or experimental program funded, authorized, or conducted by a public entity, or by a private entity acting on behalf of a public entity, may involve neurotechnology, neuromodulation, or behavioral‑control techniques without:

  (1) Prior review and approval by an independent ethics committee or institutional review board that is not under the direct control of the sponsoring agency;  
  (2) Documented, informed, written consent from each participant, provided in language they can reasonably understand; and  
  (3) A clear statement, delivered to each participant, of their right to withdraw at any time without penalty, loss of services, or loss of liberty.

(b) In no case shall individuals in the following categories be enrolled in such research or experimental programs as subjects **without additional protections and independent advocacy**:

  (1) Incarcerated persons or those in pre‑trial detention;  
  (2) Individuals on probation, parole, or under the jurisdiction of a drug court or mental‑health court;  
  (3) Persons residing in shelters, encampments, or other facilities due to homelessness;  
  (4) Persons whose legal capacity is limited by guardianship, conservatorship, or involuntary commitment.

(c) For these vulnerable populations, participation in any neurotechnology or behavioral‑control research or program:

  (1) Shall not be a condition of release, reduced sentence, access to shelter, medical care, or basic services; and  
  (2) Shall require the appointment or availability of an independent advocate or ombudsperson to monitor the individual’s interests.

(d) The intentional destruction, concealment, or unauthorized alteration of records related to any such research or program shall constitute a felony and grounds for civil liability, including a private right of action by affected individuals.

### Section 4. Relationship to Existing Neural Data Privacy Laws

(a) The rights recognized in Sections 1 through 3 are **in addition to**, and shall not be construed to limit, any protections for neural data, biometric information, or other sensitive data established under this State’s consumer privacy or data protection laws.

(b) Nothing in existing privacy statutes shall be interpreted to authorize any public entity, or any private entity acting on behalf of a public entity, to engage in practices prohibited by this amendment, including coercive neurotechnology, hypnotic loop practices, or non‑consensual mental interference.

### Legislative Findings (Optional)

(1) Advances in neurotechnology, brain–computer interfaces, and digital environments have created unprecedented abilities to monitor and influence human thought, emotion, and behavior.

(2) Existing privacy laws in this State and elsewhere primarily regulate the collection and sale of data, but do not adequately protect individuals against non‑consensual interference with their mental states, nor against coercive or experimental uses of neurotechnology by public authorities.

(3) Historical abuses, including non‑consensual behavior‑control experiments on vulnerable populations and the destruction of related records, demonstrate the need for explicit protections of mental privacy, cognitive liberty, mental integrity, and psychological continuity.

(4) It is therefore necessary to recognize neurorights in law and to prohibit coercive neurotechnology and hypnotic loop practices by public entities and their agents.
