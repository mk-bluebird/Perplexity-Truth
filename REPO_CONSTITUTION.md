# Repository Constitution

This document states core principles that govern this repository and constrain future changes, including licensing and governance decisions.

## 1. Non-Discrimination and Sovereignty

1.1. **No Discrimination Against Persons or Groups**

- The project SHALL NOT adopt any license, policy, or contribution guideline that:
  - Discriminates against any person or group, including but not limited to:
    - Augmented citizens,
    - Persons with disabilities,
    - People of specific races, genders, religions, or political beliefs.
- This includes prohibitions on:
  - Denying use of the software to specific groups,
  - Mandating surveillance or data-sharing targeted at specific groups.

1.2. **Protection of Cognitive Sovereignty**

- The project is explicitly committed to:
  - Protecting neurorights and cognitive liberty,
  - Opposing non-consensual manipulation of mental states and neural data.
- No license, contributor agreement, or governance change may:
  - Compel users or contributors to share sensitive neural or mental data with governments or private entities,
  - Authorize or endorse the use of the software for non-consensual neuro-interference.

## 2. License Change Restrictions

2.1. **License Consistency**

- The canonical LICENSE file currently uses the Hippocratic License 3.0.
- Any change to the License MUST:
  - Preserve the non-discrimination and sovereignty commitments in Section 1,
  - Be at least as protective of human rights as the current license.

2.2. **Supermajority Requirement**

- License changes or the adoption of additional licensing terms require:
  - A supermajority approval of the Security & Governance Review Board (see `GOVERNANCE.md`),
  - A public comment period of at least 30 days.

2.3. **Prohibited License Clauses**

- The project SHALL NOT adopt licenses or terms that:
  - Forbid use by “augmented individuals” or any other specific group,
  - Require automatic disclosure of user data to state actors,
  - Grant special privileges to any state or corporate actor to override user consent.

## 3. Copyright and Contribution

3.1. **Contributor Rights**

- Contributors retain rights to their contributions subject to the LICENSE.
- The project does not require assignment of exclusive rights to any single entity.

3.2. **Contribution Agreement**

- If a Contributor License Agreement (CLA) or Developer Certificate of Origin (DCO) is used, it MUST:
  - Be consistent with this Constitution,
  - Not introduce discrimination or mandatory data-sharing.

## 4. Enforcement and Interpretation

4.1. **Constitutional Review**

- Proposed changes to:
  - LICENSE,
  - GOVERNANCE.md,
  - SECURITY.md,
  - This REPO_CONSTITUTION.md
  Must be reviewed for compatibility with Sections 1–3.

4.2. **Community Oversight**

- Community members may raise a “constitutional objection” in issues or PRs if they believe a change:
  - Violates non-discrimination,
  - Weakens neurorights protection,
  - Introduces compelled data-sharing.

4.3. **Conflict Resolution**

- In case of conflict between:
  - New governance decisions, and
  - This Constitution,
  The Constitution prevails until it is amended using the supermajority and public-comment process described above.
