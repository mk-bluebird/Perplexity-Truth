# Legal Strategy and Liability Shield

This document outlines legal design choices intended to protect maintainers, contributors, and users while keeping the project open and rights-focused.

## 1. Nature of the Project

- This project is:
  - A documentation and analysis toolkit.
  - Focused on neurorights, civil liberties, and evidence mapping.
  - Not a tool for harassment, doxxing, or unlawful activity.

- All code and templates are published under the Hippocratic License 3.0, which incorporates ethical conditions against human rights abuses and harmful use.

## 2. Host Entity and Governance

- **Non-profit or non-profit-equivalent structure:**
  - Where feasible, stewardship is vested in a non-profit entity or trust whose charter emphasizes:
    - Protection of freedom of expression.
    - Defense of neurorights and civil liberties.
    - Non-discriminatory, rights-respecting documentation of alleged abuses.

- **Distributed governance:**
  - Key decisions (license changes, major policy shifts) should be transparent and, ideally, governed by a board or advisory group with expertise in:
    - Human rights law,
    - Neuroethics,
    - Digital civil liberties.

- **No central moderation of individual case content:**
  - The public repo holds:
    - Code,
    - Schemas,
    - Templates,
    - Public-law references.
  - Individual case narratives and self-reports are primarily held by users, not centrally hosted, limiting exposure.

## 3. Intermediary Protections and Jurisdictions

- **Use of intermediary shield laws:**
  - Where applicable (e.g., in the United States), the project benefits from intermediary protections that limit platform liability for user-generated content.[web:78]  
  - The project does not exercise editorial control over individual testimonies beyond technical formatting and invariant enforcement.

- **Choice of jurisdiction:**
  - Hosting and organizational domicile should favor:
    - Strong protections for freedom of expression and whistleblowing.
    - Clear safe-harbor or intermediary protections.
    - Legal systems that recognize the importance of human rights documentation.

- **No endorsement of specific allegations:**
  - The project’s role is to:
    - Structure and tier evidence,
    - Clarify what is verified vs. plausible vs. unverified,
    - Provide lawful remedy suggestions.
  - It does not claim that any particular allegation is proven unless supported by Tier 1 evidence.

## 4. User Responsibilities and Terms

- **User responsibility:**
  - Users are responsible for the content they author.
  - The project code helps them:
    - Document incidents accurately,
    - Distinguish testimony from external claims,
    - Avoid unlawful or harmful speech.

- **Terms and disclaimers:**
  - Recommend a clear TERMS file stating that:
    - The project does not provide legal, medical, or mental health advice.
    - Nothing in the toolkit constitutes legal representation.
    - Users should seek professional counsel where appropriate.

## 5. Handling Sensitive and High-Risk Content

- **No personal data in public repo:**
  - Names, addresses, and other identifying information of alleged perpetrators or victims should not be committed to the public code repository.

- **De-identification and aggregation:**
  - When sharing examples or case studies:
    - Use anonymized or aggregated data.
    - Remove direct identifiers unless individuals have given informed consent and laws allow.

- **Clear process for abuse reports:**
  - Provide a contact or issue label (e.g., `abuse-report`) for:
    - Allegations that the project or its tools are being used for harassment or defamation.
  - A small, rights-aware review group evaluates such reports and, if necessary, issues guidance without weakening protections for good-faith human-rights documentation.

## 6. DAO or Community Structures (Optional)

- **DAO with shield license (optional):**
  - If a decentralized organization is used:
    - Its charter should mirror the Hippocratic License’s ethical constraints.
    - Smart contracts should explicitly disclaim coordination of unlawful acts.
    - The DAO’s role is limited to:
      - Funding development,
      - Maintaining infrastructure,
      - Supporting research and advocacy.

- **No profit-driven data exploitation:**
  - Case data should not be monetized or used for targeted marketing.
  - Any analytics should be privacy-preserving and, where possible, based on opt-in aggregate statistics.

## 7. Litigation and Risk Management

- **Defensive documentation:**
  - Maintain clear records of:
    - Ethical guardrails in the code (invariants, safety modules).
    - Public statements in LICENSE, TERMS, and SECURITY policies.
  - These records show good-faith intent and design if legal questions arise.

- **Preferred remedies:**
  - In case of disputes, prioritize:
    - Corrections,
    - Clarifications,
    - Contextualization,
    rather than deletion or suppression of good-faith testimony.

- **No guarantees:**
  - The project cannot fully eliminate legal risk, but it can:
    - Demonstrate a consistent, rights-respecting design,
    - Provide tools that actively discourage harmful uses,
    - Keep maintainers at arm’s length from individual user allegations.

By combining ethical licensing (Hippocratic License 3.0), human-rights–aligned governance, and strong technical safeguards against misuse, the project aims to maximize protection for maintainers and users while keeping the code and methods open and usable for documenting serious neurorights and civil-liberties concerns.
